About Me

Fueled by curiosity, armed with creativity, and inspired by novelty, engineers possess a unique skill set to tackle any challenge that may arise. In addition to developing such abilities myself, I want to be an engineer to learn about our universe: how life originated on Earth, the way our planet evolved, and the means in which we can apply information from outer space to solve challenging issues in our society. Much of our knowledge of the universe is obtained through systems designed to survive harsh, unknown terrains such as robots. As an aspiring robotics engineer, I want to build such machines that can pursue otherwise impossible missions for humans.

SLAM for Robotic Maze Navigation

Created a map of an unknown environment using TurtleBot odometry, lidar scans, and ROS gmapping packages. Generated optimal path to navigate to user-specified destinations using A* path planning algorithm. Localized TurtleBot using particle filtering to handle non-Gaussian uncertainty probabilities.

Seats4U Venue Hosting Website

Deployed a website on AWS S3 that allows users to host shows and sell/purchase show tickets. Developed controllers in AWS Lambda to fetch and store user data from the SQL database to render to React UI. Transferred server and client data through designated HTTP endpoints on AWS API Gateway.

NinjaSe: A SquarePush Puzzle

Developed a solitaire SquarePush puzzle in javascript, in which an N x N square board contains squares that may have a color. Ninja-se is a character that occupies a 2x2 block of squares. The player can move Ninja-se up, down, left, or right and Ninja-se must always remain fully on the board. When Ninja-se moves, neighboring colors in the same direction are pushed into adjacent empty squares in that direction. The goal of SquarePush is to remove all colors from the board.

Research
Projects

Informing a Potential CBDC Pilot Study in New Zealand: Uncovering Public Perspectives

This project analyzes public perceptions of central bank digital currencies (CBDCs) in New Zealand, a country researching CBDCs alongside 93% of central banks worldwide. We interviewed academics, banking experts, and field-tested an online survey. We found that digital currency awareness, cybersecurity concerns, and government distrust may impact public CBDC adoption. We identified opportunities for public CBDC usage and recommended strategies to further increase and evaluate CBDC awareness.

Arterial Phantom Model

Conducted research for the manufacturing of an arterial phantom model to improve calibration for 3d ultrasound imaging Outlined detailed procedure to create metal replica of artery using silicone mold and 3D prints .

Parental Role Models: The Influence of Example

Conducted a two-sided test using data from a cross-sectional survey to assess the relationship between children of smokers versus non-smokers. At the 1% significance level, the data supported the claim that there is an association between parental smoking and child smoking. Presented results at Massachusetts Undergraduate Research Conference and BCC Undergraduate Scholars Conference.
