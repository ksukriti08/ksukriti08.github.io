About Me

Fueled by curiosity, armed with creativity, and inspired by novelty, engineers possess a unique skill set to tackle any challenge that may arise. In addition to developing such abilities myself, I want to be an engineer to learn about our universe: how life originated on Earth, the way our planet evolved, and the means in which we can apply information from outer space to solve challenging issues in our society. Much of our knowledge of the universe is obtained through systems designed to survive harsh, unknown terrains such as robots. As an aspiring robotics engineer, I want to build such machines that can pursue otherwise impossible missions for humans.

NinjaSe: A SquarePush Puzzle

Developed a solitaire SquarePush puzzle, in which an N x N square board contains squares that may have a color. Ninja-se is a character that occupies a 2x2 block of squares. The player can move Ninja-se up, down, left, or right and Ninja-se must always remain fully on the board. When Ninja-se moves, neighboring colors in the same direction are pushed into adjacent empty squares in that direction. The goal of SquarePush is to remove all colors from the board. The only way the player removes a color from the board is to form a 2x2 group of squares whose colors are all the same and request to remove that whole group.

Prepay Probability Prediction in Business

Research

Informing a Potential CBDC Pilot Study in New Zealand: Uncovering Public Perspectives

This project analyzes public perceptions of central bank digital currencies (CBDCs) in New Zealand, a country researching CBDCs alongside 93% of central banks worldwide. We conducted five interviews with academics and banking experts, 25 ad-hoc interviews with business employees, 50 public interviews, and field-tested an online survey. We found that digital currency awareness, cybersecurity concerns, and government distrust may impact public CBDC adoption. We identified opportunities for public CBDC usage and recommended strategies to further increase and evaluate CBDC awareness.
